The most common obstacle when renewing an F-6 visa is proving the Korean sponsor's income. Here's how the requirement works and what options exist if income falls short.

## The Income Standard

The Korean sponsor (your Korean spouse) must demonstrate income at or above the **standard median income** for your household size.

2026 approximate monthly thresholds:

| Household Size | Monthly Standard |
|---|---|
| 2 persons (couple) | ~KRW 3.81M |
| 3 persons (couple + 1 child) | ~KRW 4.88M |
| 4 persons (couple + 2 children) | ~KRW 5.95M |

## Accepted Income Types

- Employment income (payslip or withholding tax receipt)
- Business income (based on tax return filing)
- Rental income
- Pension income

Part-time or daily wage income is accepted if withholding tax receipts are available. Unreported income is not recognized.

## Supporting Documents

- Withholding tax receipt (prior year)
- Health insurance premium payment certificate
- Business income certificate (for self-employed)
- Certificate of employment

## When Income Is Insufficient

If the sponsor's income falls short:

1. **Asset substitution**: Property or financial assets above a certain threshold may substitute for income shortfall (check current immigration rules)
2. **Applicant's own income**: The foreign spouse's own legitimate income can be submitted
3. **Timing adjustment**: Use documentation from a higher-income year

## Renewal Timing

Apply for F-6-2 renewal within **4 months before** your current status expires. Applying after expiration constitutes illegal stay.

You must also submit proof that the marriage is ongoing — typically a joint household registration showing cohabitation.
