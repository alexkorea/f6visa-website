## Overview

The F-6 visa (Marriage Immigration) allows foreign nationals married to Korean citizens to reside in Korea. Unlike tourist visas, the F-6 must be **renewed periodically** to maintain lawful status. Failing to renew on time can result in illegal overstay status, fines, forced deportation, and future entry bans.

In 2026, immigration authorities have strengthened scrutiny to combat fraudulent marriages. Proper document preparation is more important than ever.

## When to Apply

You may submit your renewal application **up to 4 months (120 days) before your current permit expires**. It is strongly recommended to apply at least 2–3 months before expiry to allow processing time.

- Early application window: 4 months before expiry
- Standard processing time: 2–8 weeks depending on case complexity
- Late applications (after expiry): allowed within 2 months, but fines apply

## Required Documents

**Core documents (all applicants):**
- Integrated Application Form
- Passport (original + copy, valid for at least 6 months)
- Alien Registration Card (ARC)
- 1 passport photo (3×4 cm)
- Application fee: KRW 60,000

**Marriage evidence:**
- Korean spouse's family relations certificate (detailed)
- Korean spouse's marriage certificate
- Korean spouse's resident registration
- Foreign marriage certificate (apostilled or consular certified)

**Proof of genuine cohabitation:**
- Utility bills, lease agreement showing shared address
- Joint bank account statements or financial records
- Family photographs with timestamps
- Statements from neighbors, colleagues, or community members

**Additional documents for special circumstances:**
- If spouse is deceased: death certificate plus proof of marriage duration
- If divorcing: court filing documents
- If victim of domestic violence: police report and victim certificate

## Key Review Criteria

Immigration officers focus on five main factors:

1. **Genuine marital relationship** — evidence of real cohabitation and shared life
2. **Criminal background** — domestic and overseas records checked
3. **Financial stability** — combined household income must meet minimum living standards
4. **Korean language ability** — KIIP participation is viewed positively
5. **Prior immigration compliance** — any overstay history is a negative factor

## How to Apply

Visit the **Immigration Office** serving your registered address. Online pre-booking via HiKorea (www.hikorea.go.kr) is required to avoid long waits. Authorized immigration attorneys or licensed administrative agents can apply on your behalf.

## If Your Renewal Is Denied

You may file an objection within **90 days** of receiving the refusal notice. Administrative appeals and court proceedings are also available. Consult a specialist immediately — acting quickly is critical.

## After Renewal

Once renewed, your F-6 status continues with **no employment restrictions**. Remember to:
- Report any address change within 14 days
- Update ARC information if your passport is renewed
- Note your next renewal date on your calendar
