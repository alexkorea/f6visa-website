## Overview

One of the most attractive features of the F-6 (Marriage Immigration) visa is its broad employment freedom. Unlike many other visa categories, F-6 holders do **not require a separate work permit** to take employment in Korea. This means you can work in almost any industry from the day your visa is issued.

## What You Can Do

F-6 visa holders may engage in the following without restriction:

- **Office and administrative work** — corporations, NGOs, government contract positions
- **Service industry** — restaurants, retail, hotels, tourism
- **Manufacturing** — factory and production roles (except certain defense industries)
- **Education** — tutoring, language instruction, academy teaching
- **IT and creative** — development, design, content creation, marketing
- **Freelance and contract** — translation, interpretation, consulting, etc.
- **Self-employment** — registering as a sole proprietor is fully permitted

## What Is Restricted or Prohibited

Certain roles remain off-limits regardless of visa status:

| Category | Restriction |
|----------|------------|
| Civil servant positions | Korean nationals only |
| Certain defense industry roles | Security clearance and special approval required |
| Electoral and political positions | No voting or political rights for non-citizens |
| Prostitution and illegal entertainment | Immediate visa cancellation plus deportation |

## Running Your Own Business

F-6 holders can register as a **sole proprietor** with the National Tax Service (NTS). You will need your Alien Registration Card, a lease agreement for the business premises, and any industry-specific permits. Business registration typically takes 1–3 business days. Establishing a corporation is also permitted under the same terms as Korean nationals.

## Social Insurance and Tax

When employed, you are automatically enrolled in:
- **National Health Insurance** (shared cost with employer)
- **Industrial Accident Compensation** (100% mandatory, employer-funded)
- **National Pension** — subject to reciprocity agreements with your home country
- **Employment Insurance** — generally applicable; some exceptions exist

All domestic income is subject to Korean income tax. Annual tax filing is mandatory.

## Path to Permanent Residency (F-5)

Holding the F-6 visa and maintaining employment can accelerate your path to the **F-5 Permanent Residency** visa. Key requirements include:
- At least 2 continuous years in Korea after marriage
- Income or assets at or above 60% of Korea's GNI per capita
- Clean criminal record
- Basic Korean language proficiency (KIIP completion may waive the language test)

## Important Reminders

- Renew your F-6 visa on time — employment does not automatically extend your stay
- Report address changes within 14 days
- File all income taxes — same obligations as Korean nationals
- Do not facilitate illegal employment of other foreigners — severe penalties apply
