# F-6 Visa: Applying for Your Child's Residence Status

Children born in Korea to F-6 visa holders must apply for their own residency status — they are not automatically covered by a parent's visa. Apply within **90 days of birth** to avoid illegal stay status.

## Which Residence Status?

If one parent is Korean, the child qualifies for **F-2-4** (minor foreign child of Korean national). Both-foreign-parent cases vary by parental visa type.

## Required Documents
- Integrated application form
- Child's passport (issued by home country embassy)
- Birth certificate with apostille
- Parent's alien registration cards and passports
- Korean parent's family relation certificate
- Marriage certificate

## Steps
1. Get child's passport from home country embassy (allow 1–3 months)
2. Register birth (local district office or embassy)
3. Visit immigration office to apply for residence status
4. Receive alien registration card
