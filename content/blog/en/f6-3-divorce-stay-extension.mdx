# F-6-3 Visa After Divorce Korea 2026

The F-6-3 (Broken Marriage) visa allows foreign nationals to remain in Korea after their marriage to a Korean citizen ends, provided the marriage ended through no fault of the applicant.

## Qualifying Reasons for Marriage Breakdown

| Reason | Required Evidence |
|--------|-----------------|
| Death of Korean spouse | Death certificate |
| Divorce due to spouse's fault | Court judgment naming spouse's fault |
| Domestic violence | Restraining order, medical records, police report |
| Spouse's disappearance | Missing person report |

Simple mutual-consent divorce is generally **not** sufficient for F-6-3 approval.

## Required Documents

- Status change application form
- Evidence of marriage breakdown (court judgment, death certificate, etc.)
- Proof of life base in Korea (housing, income, children)
- Alien registration card, passport

## After F-6-3: Path to Permanent Residency

After sufficient residence on F-6-3, applicants may apply for F-5 (permanent residency) if they meet income and asset requirements.
