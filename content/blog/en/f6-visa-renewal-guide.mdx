# F-6 Marriage Visa Extension (Renewal) Complete Guide

**The F-6 marriage visa is typically valid for one year.** If you do not apply for an extension before it expires, you will be in violation of immigration law — resulting in departure orders, deportation, or fines. This guide explains everything you need to know: when to apply, what documents to prepare, what immigration officers review, and how to handle rejection.

<h2 id="section-1">What Is F-6 Visa Extension?</h2>

The F-6 (marriage immigrant) visa is issued to the foreign spouse of a Korean national. The initial period of stay is usually **one year**, after which it can be extended in 1–3 year increments as long as the marriage continues.

The extension is a **stay period extension** — your visa category remains F-6; you are simply renewing the duration at an Immigration Office (출입국·외국인청).

<h2 id="section-2">When to Apply</h2>

| Timeline | Details |
|----------|---------|
| **4 months before expiry** | Application window opens |
| **2–3 months before expiry** | Recommended application window |
| **By expiry date** | Absolute deadline (after = overstay) |

> **Tip:** Immigration offices often have long wait times. Book an appointment online via HiKorea 2 months before your visa expires.

<h2 id="section-3">Required Documents</h2>

**Standard documents:**
- Application for extension of sojourn period (체류기간 연장허가 신청서)
- Passport and Alien Registration Card (originals)
- Korean spouse's resident registration extract (주민등록등본)
- Family relations certificate including spouse (가족관계증명서)
- Marriage certificate (혼인관계증명서)
- Proof of cohabitation (utility bills, lease agreement, etc.)
- Application fee: KRW 60,000

**Additional documents (if applicable):**
- Spouse's income verification (pay stub, tax return, etc.)
- Recent couple photos (within 6 months)
- Children's basic certificate (if applicable)

<h2 id="section-4">Application Process</h2>

1. **Book appointment** — via HiKorea online or by phone
2. **Prepare documents** — originals and copies
3. **Visit immigration office** — go to the foreigners' registration desk
4. **Submit application** — reviewed over 2–4 weeks
5. **Receive result** — by SMS or HiKorea portal
6. **Re-issue ARC if needed** — required if card expiry date changes

<h2 id="section-5">What Reviewers Look For</h2>

The primary question is: **Is this a genuine marriage?**

- **Cohabitation:** Same registered address, utility bills, lease in both names
- **Communication:** Ability to communicate with each other (common language)
- **Financial support:** Spouse's income or combined assets
- **Children:** Having children together strengthens the case significantly

> **Important:** If you are living separately, attach a written explanation with supporting evidence. Unexplained separation is a common rejection reason.

<h2 id="section-6">Common Rejection Reasons</h2>

| Reason | How to Address |
|--------|---------------|
| Suspected fake marriage | Add more cohabitation evidence, personal statement |
| Spouse's income too low | Submit combined income or asset proof |
| Criminal record | Submit explanation; serious cases may need legal help |
| Past immigration violations | Submit explanation; consult a specialist |
| Incomplete documents | Submit additional docs and reapply |

<h2 id="section-7">FAQ</h2>

**Q. Can I stay in Korea while the extension is being processed?**
A. Yes. If you applied before your visa expired, you may remain legally in Korea during the review period.

**Q. Can I leave and re-enter instead of extending?**
A. No. An expired F-6 visa cannot be used for re-entry. You would need to apply for a new F-6 visa at a Korean consulate abroad.

**Q. Can I extend during a divorce proceeding?**
A. Extensions may be suspended or denied during divorce proceedings. If the divorce is your spouse's fault, you may be able to prove this with supporting documents.

**Q. How long will my extended visa be?**
A. Typically 1–3 years. Longer periods are often granted to couples with children or a long cohabitation history.
