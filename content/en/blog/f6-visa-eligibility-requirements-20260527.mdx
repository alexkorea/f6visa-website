# F-6 Marriage Visa Korea 2026 — Eligibility Requirements Complete Guide

The F-6 marriage immigration visa allows foreign nationals legally married to a Korean citizen to reside in Korea.
Simply being married is not enough — immigration authorities evaluate the genuineness of the marriage and the sponsor's income.
This guide covers F-6 visa eligibility requirements, income criteria, and key review points for 2026.

## Basic Eligibility Requirements

To apply for an F-6 visa, all of the following must be met:

- **Valid marriage:** Legal marriage with a Korean national, valid under both the foreign national's home country law and Korean law
- **Home country marriage registration:** Marriage must be legally registered in the foreign spouse's home country
- **Korean marriage registration:** Marriage must be registered in the Korean Family Relations Register
- **Genuine intent:** The marriage must be genuine, not merely formal

## Sponsor (Korean National) Income Requirement

The sponsor's income is one of the most critical factors in the F-6 visa review.

The sponsor must demonstrate income above a threshold based on the Ministry of Health and Welfare's median income guidelines, which vary by household size and are updated annually.
For the exact current threshold, check [Korea Immigration Service](https://www.immigration.go.kr) or consult a specialist.

If income is insufficient, a guarantor (family member of the sponsor) may supplement the income requirement, or property assets may partially compensate.

## Key Review Criteria

Immigration officers focus on:

- **Communication ability:** Whether the couple shares a common language or communication method
- **Relationship history:** How they met and the length of courtship
- **International marriage guidance:** Completion of the International Marriage Guidance Program (required for spouses from certain countries)
- **Consistency of records:** Whether marriage records in both countries match
- **Intent to settle:** Evidence of genuine intention to build a life together in Korea

## International Marriage Guidance Program

For Korean nationals marrying spouses from Vietnam, the Philippines, Cambodia, China, Uzbekistan, and certain other countries, the **Korean national** must complete the International Marriage Guidance Program (run by multicultural family support centers or Korean embassies).
Failure to complete this program may result in visa rejection.

## Required Documents (Basic)

**From the Korean Sponsor**
- Invitation letter
- Family relations certificate (including marriage)
- Resident registration certificate
- Income documentation (withholding tax certificate, business income proof, etc.)
- Criminal background check

**From the Foreign Spouse**
- Passport
- Marriage certificate from home country (with apostille and translation)
- Criminal background check from home country
- Civil status certificate (per home country format)

Additional documents may be required depending on the country and consulate. Notarization and translation requirements vary.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q1. We only had a ceremony abroad — can we still apply?**

The F-6 visa requires the marriage to be registered in the Korean Family Relations Register. A ceremony alone is not sufficient — legal registration is essential.

**Q2. If my sponsor has no job, will the visa be rejected?**

Not necessarily. A guarantor's income can supplement the sponsor's income, or property assets may partially substitute. Consult a specialist for your specific situation.

**Q3. Is re-marriage allowed?**

Yes. You'll need to provide documentation proving the termination of any previous marriage (divorce or death certificate).

**Q4. How long does processing take?**

Typically 2–4 weeks at overseas consulates. Countries with enhanced screening may take longer.
