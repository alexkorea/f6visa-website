# Complete Checklist Guide for the F-6 Spousal Visa from the Philippines

When applying for a spousal visa (F-6) for a spouse from the Philippines, the required documentation can seem overwhelming. The key is to *prove your marriage is genuine* and to provide *proper identity and credibility documents* for both the Filipino spouse and their Korean partner. Below, you'll find the essential checklist, detailed instructions, process steps, and real-world tips tailored to the unique Philippine requirements.

**Quick Summary:**  
Applicants for a Filipino spousal visa (F-6) must prepare the following documents, with several requiring notarization, apostille, or consular authentication:  
- Marriage Certificate from the Philippines (issued by the PSA)  
- PSA Certificate of Birth for the Filipino spouse  
- Korean spouse's Family and Basic Certificate  
- Police clearance and background records for the Filipino spouse  
- Proof of genuine relationship (photos/messages, etc.)  
- Translations and certified/notarized/apostilled documents  
Detailed breakdowns, tips, and common errors are covered below.

## <h2 id="section-1">What is the F-6 Marriage Visa for the Philippines?</h2>

### <h3>Definition and Who Needs It</h3>

- The **F-6 Marriage Visa** is South Korea’s key residency permit for foreign spouses, including those from the Philippines, enabling long-term stay for international couples.
- This visa allows a Filipino spouse to come and live in Korea, with further options for naturalization or visa extensions after arrival.

### <h3>Requirements and Key Points</h3>

- Marriage registration must be recognized in both countries (Philippines and Korea)
- Additional documents needed: medical report, police clearances, proof of financial ability
- **Proving your relationship is genuine** is critical

### <h3>What You Must Check When Preparing Documents</h3>
- Most official Philippine documents must be *PSA-issued*; local municipal copies are generally not accepted  
- Documents must usually be submitted within 3–6 months of issue  
- When getting apostille authentication, confirm the address, application process, and turnaround for each institution  
- <strong>Double-check that all translations are properly notarized or certified if required</strong>

## <h2 id="section-3">How to Prepare, Translate, Notarize, and Apostille Your Documents</h2>

### <h3>Document Preparation Process in the Philippines</h3>

1. Get PSA-issued documents (Marriage Certificate, Birth Certificate)
2. Obtain NBI clearance and Medical Certificate
3. Get each document apostilled or authenticated at the embassy as required
4. Translate documents from English to Korean and get them notarized, if needed
5. Obtain updated Korean identity documents (Family/Basic Certificates issued within 3 months)

### <h3>Best Practices: Translation, Notarization, and Apostille</h3>
- **Always confirm with your local embassy or immigration office the type of document authentication required before submission**

- Translation notarization should be done at Korean notary offices or law firms

### <h3>What Counts as Evidence?</h3>
- Wedding photos (ceremony, family, daily life)
- Messenger, email, and social media conversations
- List of attendees or invitations for your ceremony
- Screenshots of video calls, evidence of joint travel plans (flight or hotel bookings, etc.)

## <h2 id="section-6">Common Mistakes in the Philippine Spousal Visa Process</h2>

### <h3>Typical Errors and How to Fix Them</h3>

1. **Submitting non-PSA-issued documents**  
   - Only *PSA-certified* documents are accepted; municipal/clinic originals will be rejected

2. **Forgetting apostille or missing authentication**  
   - Apostille is required on most Philippine documents—if it’s missing, expect rejection

3. **Insufficient or poor translation and notarization**  
   - English> Korean translations should go through professional agencies

4. **Using expired documents**  
   - All documents must be recent (issued within the last 3–6 months)

5. **Not enough evidence of genuine relationship**  
   - Only a few photos or messages can be seen as suspicious or insufficient

### <h3>Checklist for Improvement</h3>

- Ensure every PSA-issued document is apostilled as required  
- Double-check that all necessary notarizations and certifications are included  
- Prepare detailed and varied evidence of your relationship

## <h2 id="section-7">FAQ – Frequently Asked Questions</h2>

### Q1. Is apostille required for all PSA-issued documents?
**A:** Yes—key documents like the marriage and birth certificates from the PSA must be apostilled, or your application will be rejected.

### Q2. Will any medical certificate from a Philippine hospital work?
**A:** It must be issued by a certified or embassy-approved hospital, and then apostilled. Check with your embassy for specific required medical tests.

### Q3. Where should I get translations and notarization done?
**A:** Use a notary office or licensed legal translator in Korea for the English-to-Korean translation and notarization.

### Q4. Are wedding photos and chat/evidence really required?
**A:** Proof of a genuine marriage is mandatory. The more variety and volume of photos, messages, call records, and travel records you provide, the better.

### Q5. How long does it take to process the F-6 spousal visa from the Philippines?
**A:** Generally 1–3 months, but it may take longer if documents are missing or if the officer requests more evidence.
