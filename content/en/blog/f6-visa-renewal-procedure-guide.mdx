# F-6 Marriage Visa Renewal Guide Korea

If you are a foreign national married to a Korean citizen, your F-6 marriage visa must be renewed before it expires. Failing to renew on time can result in illegal overstay status, departure orders, and entry bans. This comprehensive guide walks you through every step of the F-6 renewal process.

## 1. When to Apply for Renewal

You can begin the F-6 renewal application **up to 4 months before your current visa expires**. The ideal window is between 4 and 2 months prior to expiration. Applying too close to the expiry date risks processing delays that leave you in a legal gray area.

If your visa has already expired, visit the immigration office immediately. Late applications may be subject to fines and closer scrutiny.

## 2. Renewal Requirements

To successfully renew your F-6 visa, you must meet all of the following conditions:

- **Active marriage**: Your legal marriage to your Korean spouse must still be in force.
- **Cohabitation**: You must be living together with your spouse. If you reside at different addresses, you must provide a written explanation with supporting evidence.
- **Financial stability**: Either your spouse must have a qualifying income level or you must have your own verified income.
- **Law-abiding record**: No unauthorized employment or criminal violations during your current stay.

## 3. Required Documents

Prepare the following documents before visiting the immigration office.

### Basic Documents
- Integrated Application Form (available at immigration offices or via HiKorea portal)
- Original passport and copy
- Alien Registration Card (ARC)
- 1 color photo (3.5cm × 4.5cm)
- Application fee

### Marriage Verification Documents
- **Marriage Relationship Certificate** (detailed): Issued within the past 3 months
- **Family Relationship Certificate** (detailed): Issued within the past 3 months
- **Resident Registration Certificate**: Korean spouse's registration, issued within 3 months

### Financial Documents
- **Employer Certificate**: If your spouse is employed
- **Income Amount Certificate**: Issued by the National Tax Service (NTS)
- **Business Registration Certificate and Income Confirmation**: If self-employed
- **Health Insurance Premium Payment Certificate**: As supplemental income proof

### Cohabitation Evidence (if addresses differ)
- Written explanation for living apart
- Supporting documents (work or school evidence)

## 4. Where and How to Apply

### In-Person Application
Visit any Regional Immigration Office or Immigration Control Center. In Seoul, the main office is the Seoul Immigration Office in Yangcheon-gu. **Advance reservations are required** — book online at www.hikorea.go.kr or by calling 1345.

### Online Application (HiKorea)
For some applicants, online submission through the HiKorea portal is available. However, if original document verification is needed, an in-person visit may still be required.

## 5. Processing Time

Standard processing takes **2 to 4 weeks** from submission. Processing can be longer during peak periods (January, late December) or if additional documents are requested. Track your application status on the HiKorea portal.

If you need urgent processing, inquire at the immigration office about expedited options — these are not guaranteed but may be available in genuine emergencies.

## 6. Common Reasons for Rejection

Be aware of these frequent causes of F-6 renewal denial:

1. **Address mismatch**: Living at a different address from your spouse without adequate explanation
2. **Insufficient income**: Spouse's income below the minimum threshold with no alternative financial support
3. **Suspected sham marriage**: Very short marriage duration, inability to contact spouse, or lack of evidence of genuine marital life
4. **Criminal record**: Any criminal offense committed during the current stay
5. **Visa violations**: Unauthorized employment or other immigration law breaches

## 7. What to Do If Renewal Is Denied

If your renewal is denied, you can file an objection within 90 days. Submit documents that address the stated reasons for rejection. Assistance from a licensed immigration attorney or administrative agent is strongly advised.

Alternatively, you may depart Korea and reapply for the F-6 visa at a Korean embassy or consulate abroad.

## Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

**Q1. How early can I apply for F-6 visa renewal?**
A. Applications open 4 months before your visa expires. We recommend applying between 4 and 2 months before expiry.

**Q2. What if my spouse is currently unemployed?**
A. Alternative financial proof — such as bank statements, property ownership, or your own income — can substitute. Additional documents may be requested.

**Q3. Does having a Korean child help with renewal?**
A. Yes. A Korean child is strong evidence of genuine marital life and can help your case significantly. Include your Family Relationship Certificate showing the child.

**Q4. Can I complete the renewal entirely online?**
A. HiKorea allows online submission, but original document verification often requires an in-person visit, especially for first-time renewals.

**Q5. Can I travel abroad while my renewal is being processed?**
A. Departing Korea while your renewal is pending typically cancels the application. Complete your renewal first, or obtain a re-entry permit before departing.
