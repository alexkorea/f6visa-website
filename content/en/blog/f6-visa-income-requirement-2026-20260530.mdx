# F-6 Marriage Visa Sponsor Income Requirements 2026 Korea

When applying for an F-6 marriage immigration visa, the Korean citizen sponsor must demonstrate sufficient income. Based on Article 9 of the Immigration Act and the Marriage Immigration Visa Issuance Guidelines, here is a complete summary of the 2026 income requirements.

## 1. Legal Basis for Income Requirements

Under **Article 9 of the Immigration Act (Visa Issuance)**, the Minister of Justice requires that foreign nationals obtain a visa before entering Korea. Among the visa issuance criteria, the sponsor's financial capacity to support the foreign spouse is a key review item.

The Marriage Immigration Visa Issuance Guidelines (Ministry of Justice ordinance) require sponsors to have income at or above **100% of the median household income** according to household size. This process confirms that there is an adequate economic foundation for the foreign spouse to live stably in Korea.

## 2. 2026 Median Household Income Thresholds

The following are the 2026 median household income figures announced by the Ministry of Health and Welfare. The F-6 visa income requirement is **100% of the median household income or above**.

| Household Size | Monthly Median Income | Annual Equivalent |
|---------------|----------------------|-------------------|
| 2 persons | approx. ₩3,682,609 | approx. ₩44,191,308 |
| 3 persons | approx. ₩4,714,657 | approx. ₩56,575,884 |
| 4 persons | approx. ₩5,729,913 | approx. ₩68,758,956 |
| 5 persons | approx. ₩6,695,735 | approx. ₩80,348,820 |

> **Note**: The baseline is a 2-person household (sponsor + foreign spouse). If there are dependent children, the household size increases and the income threshold rises accordingly.

## 3. Required Income Verification Documents

Sponsors must submit one or more of the following documents to verify income:

- **Salaried employees**: Income tax withholding receipt, health insurance premium payment confirmation, employment certificate
- **Self-employed individuals**: Comprehensive income tax return (confirmation), business registration certificate
- **Other income**: Pension receipt confirmation, rental income documents, etc.

If the income threshold is not met, an **asset-based review** (financial assets, real estate, etc.) may substitute. Generally, holding net assets of approximately ₩68 million or more in a metropolitan area is recognized as meeting the income requirement.

## 4. Options When Income Falls Short

If you cannot meet the income requirements, consider the following approaches:

1. **Use real estate or financial assets**: Apply for asset-based review as an alternative
2. **Use a co-guarantor**: A person with sufficient income (e.g., parents) submits a personal guarantee letter
3. **Supplement income evidence**: Part-time or freelance income can be aggregated using tax invoices and contracts

Note that submitting false documents results in visa denial and future entry restrictions, so accuracy is essential.

## 5. Key Considerations During Review

- Income is assessed based on the most recent **12 months**, including bonuses and irregular income.
- Even if income falls slightly below the threshold, **stable employment** and **asset status** are considered holistically.
- Review standards may vary slightly by consulate, so it is advisable to confirm with the relevant overseas mission before applying.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q. Is the visa automatically denied if income is below the threshold?**
A. Not necessarily. The review considers supplementary documents such as asset verification or guarantor letters. Consulting an immigration specialist can help you find the best approach.

**Q. Can a dual-income couple combine their incomes?**
A. The sponsor's income is the primary basis. However, if insufficient, the foreign spouse's local income or assets may be submitted as supplementary material. Whether it is reflected in the review is at the consulate's discretion.

**Q. Can health insurance premiums be used to prove income?**
A. Health insurance payment confirmations serve as income estimation documents and, when submitted alongside a withholding receipt, strengthen the overall application.
