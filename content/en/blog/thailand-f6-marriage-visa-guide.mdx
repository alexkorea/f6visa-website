**If you are married to a Thai national and wish to live together in Korea, you will need to apply for the F-6 Marriage Immigration Visa.** The process involves legal marriage registration in both Thailand and Korea, translation and notarization of Thai marriage documents, document verification, and the visa application itself. This 2026 guide walks you through every step based on current practical requirements.

<h2 id="section-1">Thai Marriage Registration at the Amphoe</h2>

In Thailand, a legally valid marriage must be registered at a **district office (Amphoe)**. Religious ceremonies or customary marriages alone do not create legal status under Thai law. Both parties must appear in person before a registrar.

**Thai Marriage Registration Steps:**
1. Both the Korean and Thai nationals must be present at the Amphoe
2. The Korean spouse must bring a **Single Status Certificate (Certificate of No Impediment)** with appropriate authentication
3. Complete and submit the marriage registration form at the Amphoe
4. Receive the **Thai Marriage Certificate (ทะเบียนสมรส)**

**For the Korean Spouse — Single Status Certificate:**
- Obtain a Korean Marriage Relationship Certificate showing single status from a local district office or via the Government24 portal
- Have it apostilled or consularly authenticated at the Korean Embassy in Thailand
- Thai translation may be required depending on the Amphoe

> **Tip:** Requirements vary by Amphoe. Contact the specific district office in advance or consult the Korean Embassy in Thailand.

<h2 id="section-2">Korean Marriage Registration Process</h2>

After the Thai marriage is legally established, you must also register the marriage in Korea for it to be recognized under Korean law.

**Steps for Korean Marriage Registration:**
1. Prepare the Thai Marriage Certificate (original + Korean translation + notarization)
2. Gather the Korean spouse's family register documents
3. Bring the Thai spouse's passport copy
4. Visit a local district office (주민센터) or submit by mail
5. Processing time: typically 1–3 business days after submission
6. Obtain the Korean Marriage Certificate (혼인관계증명서)

> **Note:** Korean translation and notarization of the Thai Marriage Certificate are mandatory. Translation must be done by a certified translator; notarization must be obtained from a Korean notary office.

<h2 id="section-4">Translation and Notarization of Thai Marriage Certificate</h2>

The Thai Marriage Certificate (ทะเบียนสมรส) is written in Thai. For Korean marriage registration and F-6 visa applications, a **Korean translation** is required. This translation must be prepared by a certified translator and notarized at a Korean notary office.

**Process:**
1. Commission a certified translator for Thai-to-Korean translation (translator's seal and signature required)
2. Visit a Korean court notary office or notary attorney for notarization
3. Submit the original and translated document together for comparison

> **Warning:** Translations by family members or non-certified translators are not accepted as official documents.

<h2 id="section-5">Document Verification Procedure</h2>

Korean immigration authorities and consulates may require verification of the authenticity of Thai marriage documents to prevent fraudulent applications.

**Verification Methods:**
- **Apostille:** Thailand is a signatory to the Hague Convention, so Thai documents can be apostilled at the Thai Ministry of Foreign Affairs. Apostilled documents can be submitted to Korean consulates without additional consular legalization.
- **Consular Legalization:** Alternatively, the Korean Embassy in Thailand can provide consular legalization.

> **Tip:** Requirements vary by consulate. Always confirm which method is required before proceeding.

<h2 id="section-7">Common Reasons for F-6 Visa Rejection</h2>

**1. Insufficient Evidence of Genuine Relationship**
- Short courtship period or lack of meeting records
- Communication barriers (language)
- Missing chat records, photos, or visit history

**2. Income Requirements Not Met**
- Korean sponsor's income below the Ministry of Justice threshold
- Insufficient income documentation for freelancers or self-employed persons

**3. Document Errors or Omissions**
- Translation errors, missing notarization, expired documents

**4. Prior Immigration Violations**
- Thai spouse's history of overstay or unauthorized work in Korea

**5. Legal Issues with Thai Marriage**
- Failure to complete proper Thai registration or document discrepancies

> **Tip:** If your visa is rejected, reapplication is possible. Identify the specific rejection reason and prepare comprehensive supplementary documents. Working with a professional immigration agent for reapplication is strongly recommended.

<h2 id="section-8">Frequently Asked Questions</h2>

**Q1. Can I apply for F-6 if we only had a wedding ceremony in Thailand without registering at the Amphoe?**
No. The F-6 visa requires a legally valid marriage. You must complete registration at a Thai Amphoe and in Korea before applying.

**Q2. Can my Thai spouse enter Korea on a tourist visa (C-3) and convert to F-6 inside Korea?**
In principle, the F-6 visa should be applied for at an overseas Korean consulate. Status changes inside Korea may be possible under certain conditions, but not if the person is in an overstay status. Always consult a professional first.

**Q3. Can a Korean spouse who speaks no Thai complete the Thai marriage registration?**
Yes, with the help of a local interpreter or immigration consultant. Both parties must be present at the Amphoe, and documents must be in Thai.

**Q4. Does the Thai spouse's previous marriage require additional documents?**
Yes. Proof of divorce or death of a previous spouse (Thai divorce certificate or death certificate) with Korean translation and notarization is required.

**Q5. Is an interview required during F-6 visa review?**
Some consulates conduct interviews to verify the genuineness of the marriage. Both spouses should prepare consistent answers. Professional preparation guidance is recommended.
