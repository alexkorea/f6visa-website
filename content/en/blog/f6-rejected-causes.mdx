## Why rejections happen

F-6 marriage-visa rejections are rarely the result of a single fatal flaw. More often, **multiple small signals** accumulate across the application, financial proofs, family records, and consular interview, leading the Immigration Office to either request supplementary materials or refuse the visa outright.

Below are five recurring patterns Vision Admin Office has seen in real cases.

## 1. Insufficient or unverified income

Misunderstandings about the income measurement period, applicable threshold, and the family members counted in the household are frequent. Immigration is more likely to request supporting documents in cases such as:

- Missing comprehensive income-tax filings or unreported income
- Incomes consisting only of short-term, daily, or freelance work
- Sharp income swings in the 12 months before application

<Callout tone="warn" title="Practical tip">
The income threshold is updated annually by the Immigration Office, and whether co-sponsorship by parents or siblings can be used differs case by case. Confirm exact figures through a consultation.
</Callout>

## 2. Inconsistencies in the family-relation register

When the marriage is registered in Korea first, the Korean family-relation and marriage certificates must match the foreign marriage certificate and passport. Differences in romanized names, birth-date formats, and missing pre-marriage surnames are common pitfalls.

## 3. Contradictions in the consular interview

If the spouses' answers diverge on how they met, when they decided to marry, family approval, or how often they have visited each other, immigration becomes skeptical of the marriage's authenticity. Answers that grow naturally out of the couple's daily life are far stronger than answers rehearsed only on the day.

## 4. Insufficient ability to communicate

Without proof of a shared communication language between the Korean and foreign spouse, the visa can be refused. TOPIK level 1 or higher, completion of stage 1 of the Korea Immigration & Integration Program (KIIP), or evidence of study or work history in Korea by the foreign spouse all serve as supporting evidence.

## 5. Changed statements due to indecision or personal circumstances

Reversing answers during the supplementary-document phase sharply increases the probability of refusal. Even if the original submission was wrong, clearly explaining the reason for the correction with consistent facts is safer than silence or contradiction.

## Reapplication strategy

The rejection notice states a stated reason, but successful reapplication usually requires also reinforcing the **secondary signals behind it**.

- Analyze the rejection notice carefully and categorize the cause (financial / relational / testimonial / documentary)
- Add reinforcing evidence by category (additional income proof, photos and call logs of the relationship, corrected statements)
- Reapplication is generally recommended after a 3-month interval, though some causes allow immediate reapplication

## Closing

An F-6 rejection is not irreversible. Still, addressing risks at the first application — through a consultation before submitting — is the most efficient path in both time and cost. [Request a consultation](/en/contact) to receive a case-by-case diagnosis.
