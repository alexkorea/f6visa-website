# Employment Rights for F-6 Visa Holders in Korea: Complete Guide

## Employment Rights Under the F-6 Visa

F-6 (Marriage Immigrant) visa holders may work freely in Korea without a separate work permit. The F-6 status imposes no restrictions on employment, allowing holders to enter into labor contracts and receive wages on equal terms with Korean nationals.

## Eligible Occupations

F-6 holders may work in virtually any occupation—full-time, part-time, or contract employment. Professional roles such as doctors, lawyers, and teachers are accessible upon obtaining the relevant Korean qualification.

## Employment Process

1. Find a job through recruitment platforms or employment agencies
2. Present your alien registration card when signing a contract
3. Employer registers you for employment insurance, health insurance, and national pension
4. Begin work

## Income Tax Obligations

F-6 holders earning income in Korea must file income taxes. Wages are withheld at source, and an annual comprehensive income tax return is filed every May.

## Maternity and Childcare Benefits

Employed F-6 holders receive the same maternity and childcare protections as Korean nationals under the Employment Insurance Act, including maternity leave and parental leave.
