Foreign spouses holding an F-6 marriage immigration visa can work in Korea without a separate work permit.

Under **Immigration Control Act Enforcement Decree Schedule 1-2**, F-6 status holders are permitted to engage in employment activities within the scope of their residency status — covering full-time, part-time, contract, and freelance work.

This guide explains the scope of employment permitted on an F-6 visa, what to watch out for, and how to enroll in health insurance and national pension — updated for 2026.

## Can You Work on an F-6 Visa?

Yes — **F-6 marriage visa holders can work in Korea freely without a separate work permit.**

The F-6 status is a residential-type visa based on marriage to a Korean national. Unlike a tourist visa, it permits broad economic activity.

Permitted employment types include:
- Full-time employment (including indefinite contracts)
- Contract-based employment
- Part-time and hourly work
- Freelance activities
- Operating a business as a sole proprietor or company

Note: Certain positions restricted to Korean nationals by law (e.g., civil service, specific licensed professions) are not available to F-6 holders.

## What Employers Need to Do When Hiring F-6 Visa Holders

**1. Verify Visa Status**

Before hiring, confirm the alien registration card shows F-6 status. No special work permit is required.

**2. Enroll in Employment Insurance**

F-6 holders are subject to mandatory employment insurance (고용보험) enrollment, which provides unemployment benefits.

**3. Enroll in Industrial Accident Insurance**

All employees including foreign nationals must be enrolled in industrial accident insurance (산재보험).

**4. Issue a Written Employment Contract**

A written employment contract must be prepared and provided in a language the worker understands.

## Health Insurance Enrollment

**If employed (Workplace Health Insurance):**
- Employer registers you automatically with the National Health Insurance Service
- Premiums are split equally between employer and employee
- Medical benefits are available immediately upon enrollment

**If not employed (Regional Health Insurance):**
- Self-register at the nearest National Health Insurance Service branch
- Premiums are calculated based on income and assets

Visit [National Health Insurance Service](https://www.nhis.or.kr) for premium calculations and coverage information.

## National Pension Enrollment

F-6 visa holders are generally required to join the National Pension Service.

- **Employed:** Automatically enrolled as a workplace subscriber; premiums split between employer and employee
- **Not employed:** Can enroll voluntarily as a regional subscriber
- **Returning home:** Lump-sum refund may be available upon departure (depending on social security agreement with your home country)

Check [National Pension Service](https://www.nps.or.kr) for details on contributions and refund eligibility.

## Important Cautions When Working on F-6

**1. Keep Your F-6 Visa Renewed**

Being employed does not automatically renew your F-6 visa. Renew at least 4 months before expiry. Missing renewal while employed can result in unauthorized stay — a serious violation.

**2. Divorce Affects Your F-6 Status**

The F-6 visa is based on the marital relationship. If you divorce, your status may need to change. Certain circumstances (caring for a child, fault on spouse's side) allow stay extension — consult a specialist immediately if facing divorce.

**3. Income Tax Obligations**

Employment income is subject to Korean income tax. Verify that your employer is withholding taxes properly, and confirm whether you need to file an annual income tax return.

## Using Employment Income to Qualify for F-5 Permanent Residency

Stable employment income is one of the strongest factors in F-5 (permanent residency) applications.

- **Minimum F-6 holding period:** Generally 2 years with maintained marriage and Korea residence
- **Employment record at F-5 application time:** Counted as proof of financial self-sufficiency
- **Korean language ability:** TOPIK Level 1 or KIIP program completion recommended

Optimal timing for F-5 application varies — consult an expert to plan your timeline.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q1. Can I do part-time work on an F-6 visa?**

Yes. All forms of employment including part-time work are permitted. No separate work permit is needed.

**Q2. Does working affect my F-6 visa renewal?**

Employment history actually helps at renewal by proving financial self-sufficiency. It does not negatively affect renewal.

**Q3. Can a foreign national register a business in Korea on F-6?**

Yes. After completing alien registration, F-6 holders can register as a sole proprietor at the local tax office.

**Q4. If I get divorced while employed, does my visa get canceled?**

F-6 status is not automatically canceled upon divorce, but a status change review is required. Certain cases (child custody, fault on spouse) allow continued stay — seek specialist advice immediately.

**Q5. Does employment income help with F-5 permanent residency applications?**

Yes. Stable employment income is a key indicator of financial self-sufficiency, which positively affects F-5 applications.
