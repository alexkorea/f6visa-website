## F-6 Visa Status After Divorce

The F-6 visa is based on marriage to a Korean national. After divorce, you may still maintain your stay under certain conditions.

## Conditions to Maintain Stay After Divorce

Under immigration regulations, you must prove at least one of the following:

1. **Child custody:** You are raising a child born with your Korean ex-spouse
2. **Fault of the Korean spouse:** The marriage ended due to the Korean spouse's fault (domestic violence, infidelity, etc.)
3. **Sufficient marriage duration:** Evidence that the marriage was maintained in good faith for a reasonable period

## Recommended Status Change

If conditions are met, consider changing from F-6 to F-2 (residence) or F-5 (permanent residence).

## Important Note

Immigration authorities may review your F-6 status once they learn of the divorce. Contact Vision Administrative Office immediately after divorce.
