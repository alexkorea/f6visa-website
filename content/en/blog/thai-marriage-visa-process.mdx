## F-6 Marriage Visa for Thailand: Essential Requirements and Eligibility
**Related keyword:** Thai spouse eligibility

### What is the F-6 Visa?
The F-6 marriage visa allows a foreign spouse, married to a Korean national, to legally reside and live in Korea.

### Who is Eligible for the Thai F-6 Marriage Visa?
- Thai nationals legally married to a South Korean citizen
- Marriage must be officially registered and a marriage certificate issued
- The paperwork you’ll need may vary depending on whether you reside in Korea or abroad

### Recent Changes Worth Noting
Immigration authorities have become much stricter in reviewing not just paperwork but also the authenticity and the substance of your married life.
> **Note:** Marriage certificates alone might not be enough. Verify with an expert what supplementary documents might apply to your specific case.

### Key Points in Thai Marriage Visa Screening
- A comprehensive review is made of the applicant’s marriage authenticity, background, and financial stability
- Income and housing requirements are updated every year

> **Pro Tip:** Income and housing requirements change annually—double-check the criteria for this year in a consultation before you apply.

## Detailed List of Required Documents for Thai Marriage Visa
**Related keyword:** required documents

### Documents Checklist (for applications filed in Korea)

| Item                     | Document                                    | Notes                        |
|--------------------------|---------------------------------------------|------------------------------|
| Marriage certificate     | Issued after marriage registration in Korea | Submit the original           |
| Family relation certificate | Issued by Korean applicant’s family       |                              |
| Thai marriage certificate| Issued after marriage registered in Thailand | Must be translated and notarized |
| Thai spouse ID           | Thai passport, national ID                   | Must be translated and notarized |
| Basic certificate        | Issued by Korean applicant                   |                              |
| Photo                    | Recent passport photo, 3.5 x 4.5 cm         | One or two copies             |
| Financial docs           | Income certificate, tax withholding statements | Check current requirements   |
| Housing documentation    | Lease, property deed, etc.                  | Proof of actual residence  |
| Others                   | Proof of genuine marriage, family photos    | Additional docs may be requested|

> **Checklist:**
- All Thai documents must be translated into Korean, notarized (apostille) or certified by the Korean embassy
- Use documents issued within the last 90 days
- Missing paperwork may result in requests for additional documents
- Support your marriage authenticity—provide joint photos, chat history, family event docs, etc.

> **Practical tip:** Questions about Thai document translation, notarization, and apostille come up most often. The best approach depends on your case—get tailored advice if you’re unsure.
