# Complete Guide to the F-6 Spouse Marriage Visa Process and Documents for Uzbekistan Nationals

A spouse holding Uzbekistan citizenship can apply for an F-6 (marriage immigration) visa to reside long-term in Korea after completing legally valid marriage registration in both countries with their Korean spouse.
The F-6 visa is issued to foreign spouses of Korean nationals, and requires that the marriage be legally recognized in both countries and that the sponsoring spouse meets income and housing requirements.
This guide covers everything from preparing documents in Uzbekistan to the Korean visa application process, review criteria, and frequently asked questions — step by step.

<h2 id="section-1">Understanding the F-6 Marriage Visa for Uzbekistan Nationals</h2>

### What Is the F-6 Visa?

The F-6 (marriage immigration) visa is a long-term residence status issued to foreign spouses in a legally recognized marriage with a Republic of Korea national.
It is governed under **Article 10 of the Immigration Act and the classification of residence status in Annexed Table 1**, with an initial stay period of one year that can be extended through renewal.
Uzbekistan is a member of the Commonwealth of Independent States (CIS) and maintains consular cooperation with Korea; it is included among the countries eligible for visa issuance by the [Korea Immigration Service](https://www.immigration.go.kr).

> **Practical tip:** Unlike a standard visitor visa (B-2) or short-term visa (C-3), the F-6 visa allows the holder to work in Korea without a separate work visa, and also grants eligibility to enroll in health insurance and other social insurance programs.

### F-6 Visa Types at a Glance

| Type | Eligible | Key Requirements |
|------|----------|-----------------|
| F-6-1 | Spouse of a Republic of Korea national | Marriage registration completed in both countries; income and housing requirements met |
| F-6-2 | Parent raising a child born to a Korean national | Applicable when raising a child following divorce |
| F-6-3 | Victim of marriage breakdown due to the other spouse's fault | Issued for protection purposes such as domestic violence cases |

For an Uzbekistan spouse applying for a marriage visa for the first time, the **F-6-1** type applies.

<h2 id="section-2">Marriage Registration Process in Both Countries</h2>

### Registering the Marriage in Uzbekistan

If the marriage is registered in Uzbekistan first, you will apply for registration at the civil registry office (ZAGS) in your area of residence.
The **marriage certificate** issued by ZAGS must then receive Apostille certification and a certified Korean translation before it can be submitted to Korean authorities.
As Uzbekistan is a signatory to the Hague Convention, Apostille certification allows official documents to be legally recognized abroad.

> **Important:** The Uzbekistan marriage certificate must have a proper **Apostille certification**.
> A simple notarization or consular confirmation alone may not be accepted by Korean authorities in all cases, so confirm the specific requirements with the relevant institution in advance.

### Registering the Marriage in Korea

After completing the marriage registration in Uzbekistan, submit a marriage registration form to the local district or municipal office (si/gu/eup/myeon office) in the Korean spouse's area of registered residence.
Required documents include the Apostille-certified Uzbekistan marriage certificate, a certified Korean translation, and a copy of the Uzbekistan spouse's passport.
Once the foreign spouse is officially registered in the Korean Family Relations Register, the key legal requirement for the F-6 visa application is satisfied.
Processing times and additional document requirements may vary by district office, so it is advisable to confirm in advance before visiting.

<h2 id="section-3">Documents to Prepare in Uzbekistan</h2>

### Required Document Checklist

The main documents that need to be prepared locally in Uzbekistan are as follows:

- **Marriage certificate** (Apostille certification + certified Korean translation)
- **Birth certificate** (Apostille certification + certified Korean translation)
- **Criminal record clearance certificate** (issued by the Ministry of Internal Affairs of Uzbekistan; Apostille certification required)
- **Passport** (with at least 6 months of validity remaining)
- **Passport-size photo** (3.5cm × 4.5cm, taken within the past 6 months)
- **Medical certificate** (issued by a medical institution designated by the Ministry of Justice; confirm whether required at time of application)

> **Practical tip:** The criminal record clearance certificate has a set validity period from the date of issue.
> Obtaining it too early may mean it expires before the visa application is submitted, so the order and timing of document preparation is critical.
> There have been cases of application delays caused by exactly this issue, so it is strongly recommended to coordinate your schedule with a professional in advance.

### Notes on Translation and Notarization

All official Uzbekistan documents must be submitted together with a **certified Korean translation**.
Translation and notarization can be arranged through a Korean notary office or through the Embassy of the Republic of Korea in Tashkent.
Fees vary depending on the type and volume of documents; exact costs differ by case and will be clearly explained during a free consultation.

<h2 id="section-4">Documents to Prepare in Korea (Sponsoring Spouse)</h2>

### Required Document Checklist for the Sponsoring Spouse

The Korean spouse (sponsor) must prepare the following documents:

- **Family relations certificate** (detailed copy, issued within the past 3 months)
- **Marriage relations certificate** (detailed copy, issued within the past 3 months)
- **Certificate of resident registration** (issued within the past 3 months)
- **Certificate of employment or business registration certificate**
- **Income verification documents** (National Health Insurance premium payment confirmation, income amount certificate, wage and salary income withholding receipt, etc.)
- **Housing-related documents** (lease agreement, building registry, etc.)
- **Invitation letter** (completed using the official immigration-designated form)
- **Personal guarantee letter** (official immigration-designated form)

> **Important:** Income requirement thresholds change every year.
> For the exact income threshold applicable this year and the income verification method appropriate for your situation, please confirm with a professional before proceeding.

### Income and Housing Requirements

The **sponsoring spouse's income requirement** is one of the most heavily weighted criteria in the F-6 visa review.
If income falls short of the required threshold, alternatives such as combined household income or asset verification may be worth exploring.
The housing requirement is satisfied by securing an independent living space where both spouses can reside together.
Since the applicable criteria and required documents can vary by individual circumstances, it is recommended to review your specific situation with a professional in advance.

<h2 id="section-5">Step-by-Step F-6 Visa Application Process</h2>

### Overview of the Full Process

| Step | Processing Authority | Key Actions |
|------|---------------------|-------------|
| Step 1 | ZAGS (Civil Registry Office), Uzbekistan | Local marriage registration and issuance of marriage certificate |
| Step 2 | Apostille and notarization authorities, Uzbekistan | Apostille certification of documents and certified Korean translation |
| Step 3 | Local district/municipal office, Korea | Korean marriage registration and Family Relations Register completion |
| Step 4 | Embassy of the Republic of Korea in Tashkent, Uzbekistan | F-6 visa application submission and consular review |
| Step 5 | Korea Immigration Service | Foreigner registration and residence card issuance after entry into Korea |

### Submitting the Visa Application and the Review Process

The spouse residing in Uzbekistan must visit the **Embassy of the Republic of Korea in Tashkent** in person to apply for the F-6 visa.
Following document submission, a consular review takes place, and requests for additional documents or an interview may occur.
Processing times vary depending on when the application is submitted and the specifics of each case, and may differ between immigration offices and consulates — confirm in advance where possible.

> **Practical tip:** In recent cases, applicants who submitted complete documentation were still called in for additional interviews.
> Guidance on expected interview questions and how to prepare will be provided separately during consultation.

<h2 id="section-6">Genuine Relationship Assessment and Key Considerations</h2>

### What the Genuineness Assessment Involves

The [Korea Immigration Service](https://www.immigration.go.kr) verifies the **genuineness of the marriage** as a core criterion during the F-6 visa review.
To demonstrate a genuine relationship, it is practically recommended to submit documentation of your relationship history (messages, photos), family introduction photos, and travel records between both countries (passport entry/exit stamps, etc.).
The relevant legal basis is **Annex 5 of the Enforcement Rules of the Immigration Act** and Ministry of Justice notices; the applicable legislation can be reviewed directly at the [Korea Law Information Center](https://www.law.go.kr).

### Age Gap and Repeated International Marriage History

The Korean government may apply heightened review standards to cases involving a significant age gap or a history of multiple international marriages.
If your situation falls under these categories, you must prepare more thorough evidence of a genuine relationship, and a review of eligibility requirements is essential.
Whether your specific circumstances trigger these standards and what steps to take must be confirmed through professional consultation in advance.
There have been recent changes in how related regulations are applied in practice, so accurate determination of how they apply to your situation requires professional verification.
You can also check the latest visa guidance at [HiKorea](https://www.hikorea.go.kr).

<h2 id="section-7">FAQ: Uzbekistan Marriage Visa — Common Questions</h2>

**Q1. Do I need to register the marriage in Uzbekistan first, or in Korea first?**

Either order is legally permissible, but in practice, registering in Uzbekistan first and then registering in Korea is the more commonly used approach.
Which order is more advantageous depends on your current residency status (whether you are residing in Uzbekistan or temporarily staying in Korea, etc.), so it is best to consult in advance to determine the optimal path for your situation.

**Q2. If the Uzbekistan spouse is currently staying in Korea, is it possible to change visa status to F-6?**

In some cases, it is possible to change residence status from within Korea to F-6 if the person entered on a short-term status (C-3, B-2, etc.).
However, this is not permitted in all situations — eligibility depends on the current visa status and the circumstances of entry.
Please share your current situation with a professional for a thorough review and tailored guidance.

**Q3. Does failing to meet the income requirement automatically result in a visa denial?**

Not meeting the income requirement does not automatically lead to a denial; alternatives such as asset verification or combined household income may be worth exploring.
The exact income threshold applicable this year and the alternative approaches suited to your situation can be clarified through consultation.

**Q4. Can an F-6 visa holder work in Korea?**

F-6 visa holders are permitted to work in Korea without obtaining a separate work visa.
For the detailed scope of permitted employment activities and any reporting obligations, please check the latest guidelines from the [Korea Immigration Service](https://www.immigration.go.kr) or contact your local immigration office directly.

**Q5. When and how do I renew the F-6 visa?**

The F-6 visa renewal application must be submitted before the current stay period expires. You can apply online through [HiKorea](https://www.hikorea.go.kr) or in person at your local immigration office.
Income and housing requirements are re-evaluated at renewal, and the list of required documents varies by individual circumstances — details will be provided during consultation.

**Q6. Where can I obtain the criminal record clearance certificate from Uzbekistan?**

It is issued by an agency under the Ministry of Internal Affairs (National Police Agency) of Uzbekistan, and must receive **Apostille certification** after issuance.
The issuance process and timeline may vary by region; you can also obtain procedural guidance through the Embassy of the Republic of Korea in Tashkent.
