## What is the F-6-1 Marriage Immigration Visa?

The F-6-1 visa is a marriage immigration stay qualification established under Article 10 of the Immigration Control Act and Appendix 1, Item 28-2 of its Enforcement Decree. It is issued to spouses of Korean nationals when the marriage is genuine and normal. Applicants must meet the screening criteria under Article 9-2 of the Enforcement Regulations.

## Common Required Documents (All Countries)

The following documents are required for all applicants regardless of nationality.

1. **Visa application form** — Official form from the Ministry of Foreign Affairs or overseas mission
2. **Valid passport** — 6+ months validity, at least 1 blank visa page
3. **Passport-size photo** — Color, 3.5cm × 4.5cm, taken within 6 months
4. **Korean spouse's family relationship certificate** — Detailed version, issued within 3 months
5. **Marriage relationship certificate** — Detailed version, issued within 3 months
6. **Statement of how the couple met** — Describing the meeting circumstances, courtship, and decision to marry
7. **Proof of relationship** — KakaoTalk/text screenshots, photos, flight tickets, hotel receipts, etc.
8. **Financial proof** — National health insurance payment confirmation, income certificate, employment certificate
9. **Criminal background check** — For the Korean spouse (required by some missions)

## Documents from Foreign Spouse's Home Country

The foreign spouse must prepare the following documents from their home country.

- **Marriage registration proof**: Marriage certificate per local law (apostille or Korean mission authentication)
- **Criminal record check**: Issued by home country police or official agency (Korean translation + notarization)
- **Birth certificate**: Required by some countries (notarized translation required)
- **Divorce documents**: If previously married, divorce certificate (notarized translation)

## Financial Requirements

The Korean spouse's financial capacity is reviewed based on income standards (at least 100% of the median income standard) under the Enforcement Regulations of the Immigration Control Act.

- **Employed**: Health insurance payment confirmation, employment certificate, income withholding receipt
- **Self-employed**: Comprehensive income tax filing confirmation, VAT return
- **Insufficient income**: Guarantor system may be utilized (guarantor's income documents required)

## Proof of Relationship

Under Article 9-2 of the Enforcement Regulations, proving the genuineness of the marriage is critical.

- KakaoTalk or SNS message printouts
- Video call history screenshots
- Mutual visit flight tickets and accommodation receipts
- Wedding photos
- Proof of cohabitation (if applicable)

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q. Does registering the marriage in Korea make the visa application easier?**
A. Registering the marriage at a Korean community center (dong office) places the foreign spouse's information on the Korean marriage certificate, which plays an important role in proving marriage genuineness during visa screening.

**Q. Can the visa be rejected if the courtship period is short?**
A. The genuineness of the relationship matters more than its length. Even with a short courtship, adequate communication records and a detailed statement can support approval.
