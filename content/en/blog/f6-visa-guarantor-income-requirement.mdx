# Complete Guide to Using a Guarantor for Your Marriage Visa — Solutions When the F-6 Income Requirement Falls Short

**Even if you don't meet the income requirement for the F-6 marriage visa, you can still apply by designating a qualified personal guarantor to supplement the income shortfall.**
This option is most commonly used when the Korean spouse is currently unemployed, is a freelancer or self-employed and has difficulty proving income, or earns below the required threshold.
This guide walks you through the legal basis of the guarantor system, eligibility requirements, required documents, the application process, and key practical considerations you need to know at every step.

<h2 id="section-1">What Is the F-6 Marriage Visa Income Requirement?</h2>

### Understanding the Basics

The F-6 marriage visa (marriage immigration) requires the **Korean spouse (the petitioner/sponsor) to demonstrate sufficient income** to ensure the foreign spouse can live stably in Korea.
In accordance with notices issued by the [Korea Immigration Service (Ministry of Justice)](https://www.immigration.go.kr) and the Enforcement Rules of the Immigration Control Act, the petitioner must prove a minimum annual income level.
The income threshold is subject to change each year based on official Ministry of Justice notices, and failure to meet it may result in visa denial.

> **Important:** The exact income threshold changes annually according to Ministry of Justice notices.
> Always check the latest figures directly on the [Korea Immigration Service website](https://www.immigration.go.kr) or consult a professional before applying.

### Common Situations Where Proving Income Is Difficult

There are many scenarios in which meeting the income requirement can be challenging:

- The Korean spouse is currently unemployed or actively job-seeking
- The Korean spouse is a freelancer or self-employed and lacks sufficient income documentation
- The Korean spouse is employed but earns below the required annual income
- The Korean spouse recently changed jobs or started a business and has a short income history
- The Korean spouse is on parental leave, medical leave, or has otherwise experienced a temporary income reduction

In these situations, the guarantor system serves as an important supplementary option.

<h2 id="section-2">Legal Basis for the F-6 Marriage Visa Guarantor System</h2>

### Relevant Laws and Institutional Background

The marriage visa guarantor (personal guarantor) system is grounded in **provisions of the Immigration Control Act related to personal guaranty, which can be verified through the [National Law Information Center](https://www.law.go.kr).**
A guarantor supplements the petitioning Korean spouse's income shortfall and legally vouches for the foreign spouse's stay in Korea.
Under the law, a guarantor may be held liable for costs such as deportation expenses if the guaranteed party (the foreign spouse) becomes subject to an administrative measure such as forced departure.

> **Practical Tip:** The guarantor system is a *supplement* to insufficient income — not a *replacement* for it.
> Immigration officers evaluate the guarantor's financial capacity alongside the petitioner's overall situation. Simply having a guarantor does not guarantee automatic approval.

### Visa Types Where the Guarantor System Can Be Used

| Visa Category | Guarantor Available? | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| F-6-1 (Marriage Immigration) | Yes | Used to supplement income shortfall |
| F-6-2 (Spouse of a National) | Yes | Subject to case-by-case review |
| F-6-3 (Child-Rearing) | Conditionally | At the discretion of the reviewing officer |
| Extension of Stay | Yes | Same requirements as the initial application |

<h2 id="section-3">Eligibility Requirements for an F-6 Marriage Visa Guarantor</h2>

### Who Can Serve as a Guarantor

Not just anyone can serve as a guarantor. Per the [Korea Immigration Service](https://www.immigration.go.kr), a guarantor must meet all of the following requirements:

- Must be a **South Korean national** or a **permanent resident (F-5 visa holder)**
- Priority consideration is given to **immediate family members or relatives** of the Korean spouse (the petitioner)
- Must be able to **demonstrate stable income at or above the required level** with supporting documents
- Must have **no disqualifying factors**, such as outstanding tax delinquencies or a criminal record
- Must have **a track record of faithfully fulfilling previous guarantor obligations**, if applicable

> **Important:** The income standard for guarantors may change annually.
> If the guarantor's income does not meet the current threshold, the guaranty may not be recognized. Always seek professional review in advance.

### Practical Considerations When Selecting a Guarantor

Confirming income eligibility alone is not enough when selecting a guarantor.
Immigration authorities conduct a comprehensive review that includes the guarantor's employment stability, tax payment history, and asset status.
In recent practice, there has been a growing number of cases where the type of income (employment income vs. business income) affects whether the guaranty is accepted.
Since the appropriate selection criteria depend on your specific circumstances, it is advisable to review your options with a professional.

### Essential Documents for the Guarantor

If you are using a guarantor, the following documents must be submitted in addition to the standard F-6 application documents.
Always check [Hi Korea](https://www.hikorea.go.kr) for the most current document checklist and official forms.

| Document | Issuing Authority | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Personal Guaranty Form | Designated form from the immigration office | Guarantor's original signature and seal required |

| Guarantor's Certificate of Resident Registration | Local community center / Government24 | Issued within the last 3 months |
| Guarantor's Income Certificate | Tax office / National Tax Service (Hometax) | Based on the most recent tax year |
| Certificate of Employment Income Withholding | Employer (for salaried employees) | Varies by income type |
| Certificate of Employment | Current employer | Required for salaried employees |
| Business Registration Certificate (copy) | Jurisdictional tax office | Required for self-employed individuals |
| Health Insurance Premium Payment Confirmation | National Health Insurance Service | Used as supplementary income reference |

> **Practical Tip:** Required documents may vary depending on the application type (initial application, extension, or status change) and the handling immigration office.
> Always confirm the latest document requirements with [Hi Korea](https://www.hikorea.go.kr) or your local immigration office before submitting.

<h2 id="section-5">Step-by-Step Guarantor Application Process</h2>

### Step 1: Pre-Verify the Guarantor's Eligibility

Before designating a guarantor, the first step is to **confirm that the candidate meets all eligibility requirements.**
Check in advance whether they satisfy the income threshold, have a clean tax payment record, and have no history of delinquency.
Submitting an application with an ineligible guarantor can work against you during review and may significantly extend the processing time.

### Step 2: Gather Documents and Complete the Personal Guaranty Form

Once the guarantor is confirmed, obtain the Personal Guaranty Form from your local immigration office or download it from [Hi Korea](https://www.hikorea.go.kr).
The guarantor must personally sign and seal the form, and all supporting documents listed above must be prepared without exception.
Missing documents or errors in the form are among the most common causes of delays or rejections.

### Step 3: Submit Your Application and Await Review

Submit the completed application package to your local immigration office (for domestic applications) or the relevant Korean embassy or consulate (for overseas applications).
Review timelines vary by immigration office, and you may be asked to provide additional documents during the process.
Processing times differ by location, so it is important to work with a professional in advance to develop the most efficient strategy for your situation.

<h2 id="section-6">Key Cautions and Practical Tips When Using a Guarantor</h2>

### Legal Obligations the Guarantor Assumes

Becoming a guarantor is not a simple act of signing paperwork.
**Under the Immigration Control Act, a guarantor may be held legally responsible for costs such as the foreign spouse's departure expenses or forced deportation costs.**
Anyone considering becoming a guarantor must fully understand these legal obligations before agreeing, and it is advisable to obtain legal clarification on the scope of these responsibilities in advance.

> **Important:** Immigration authorities have been enforcing guarantor obligations more strictly in recent years.
> A history of failing to fulfill guarantor obligations can restrict a person's ability to serve as a guarantor in the future, so be sure to verify this before proceeding.

### When a Guarantor Alone Is Not Enough

There are situations where visa approval may remain difficult even with a guarantor in place:

- Both the petitioner and the foreign spouse have no income whatsoever
- The guarantor's income also falls below the required threshold
- There is a prior history of immigration violations or unlawful stay
- Concerns have been raised about the authenticity of the marriage during the review process

In these situations, you may need to submit additional supporting materials beyond the guarantor arrangement.
In similar recent cases, positive outcomes were achieved by also submitting asset verification documents alongside a written statement attesting to the authenticity of the marriage.
The right approach for your specific circumstances should always be determined through a professional consultation.

### Other Ways to Supplement an Income Shortfall

In addition to a guarantor, there are other options for addressing an income shortfall:

- **Asset verification:** Partial supplementation may be possible by documenting assets such as real estate, savings accounts, or fixed deposits
- **Letter of Upcoming Employment:** May be used if the petitioner has secured a job offer (subject to the reviewing officer's discretion)
- **Including the Foreign Spouse's Income:** If the foreign spouse has lawful income, it may be partially counted toward the requirement

However, whether any of these alternatives will be accepted depends on the reviewing officer and the policies of the relevant immigration office.
Choosing the most effective approach requires working with a professional to develop a strategy tailored to your situation.

<h2 id="section-7">Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)</h2>

### Q1. Can I use a parent or sibling as my guarantor?

**Yes, you can.**
A parent, sibling, or other immediate family member or relative of the Korean spouse can serve as a guarantor, provided they meet all eligibility requirements.
That said, be sure to verify the candidate's income level and confirm there are no disqualifying factors before proceeding.

### Q2. Does having a guarantor completely waive the income requirement?

**No, it does not result in a full waiver.**
A guarantor is a *supplementary* measure to address an income shortfall — the immigration officer evaluates the petitioner's and guarantor's situations together as a whole.
You may apply using a guarantor even if the petitioner has no income at all, but whether your application is approved depends on the specifics of your case.

### Q3. What is the specific income threshold required for a guarantor?

The income standard for guarantors changes annually based on Ministry of Justice notices.
For the current year's exact figures, check the official announcements on the [Korea Immigration Service website](https://www.immigration.go.kr) or consult a professional to confirm the latest requirements.

### Q4. Can assets alone substitute for the income requirement?

Asset documentation can serve as partial supplementary evidence for an income shortfall, but **assets alone generally cannot fully substitute for the income requirement.**
Combining asset documentation with a guarantor tends to be more effective, and since the outcome varies by case, professional review is recommended.

### Q5. How long does the review process take after submitting a guarantor application?

Processing times vary depending on the immigration office handling your case, the time of year, and whether all required documents were submitted in order.
If additional documents are requested, the timeline may extend further.
Since strategies for shortening the processing time depend on your specific situation, consulting with a professional to develop an optimal plan is advisable.

### Q6. Can I use the guarantor system when applying for F-6 sponsorship from outside Korea?

**Yes, you can.**
If the foreign spouse is currently abroad, the guarantor system can also be used when applying for sponsorship through a Korean embassy or consulate.
Since required documents and procedures can vary by diplomatic mission, be sure to check with the relevant embassy or consulate in advance.
