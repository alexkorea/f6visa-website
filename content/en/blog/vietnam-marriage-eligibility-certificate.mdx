# Complete Guide to Vietnam Certificate of Marriage Eligibility — Seoul vs Busan Consulate Differences

**The Vietnam Certificate of Marriage Eligibility (Giấy xác nhận tình trạng hôn nhân) is one of the most critical documents for F-6 marriage visa applications in Korea.** Without this document, completing the marriage registration in Korea is impossible. Since it can only be issued by the Vietnamese consulate in Korea, understanding the differences between the Seoul and Busan consulates is essential for efficient planning.

<h2 id="section-1">What Is the Vietnam Certificate of Marriage Eligibility?</h2>

The Vietnam Certificate of Marriage Eligibility is an official document issued by the Vietnamese government confirming that a Vietnamese national is legally eligible to marry. In Vietnamese, it is called **"Giấy xác nhận tình trạng hôn nhân"** or **"Giấy xác nhận đủ điều kiện kết hôn."** It certifies that the applicant is currently unmarried (or legally free to remarry) and has no legal impediment to marriage under Vietnamese law.

This document is **required by Korean law for marriage registration at a local district office (주민센터)** involving a foreign national, and it also serves as a key document in the subsequent F-6 visa application process.

> **Important:** Without this certificate, marriage registration in Korea is not possible. It must be obtained before applying for the F-6 visa.

---

<h2 id="section-2">Issuing Authority: Vietnamese Consulate in Korea</h2>

The Certificate of Marriage Eligibility is an official Vietnamese government document, so it can only be issued by **Vietnamese consular offices in Korea**. Korean government agencies cannot issue this document.

There are two Vietnamese consular offices in Korea:

| Consulate | Location | Jurisdiction |
|---|---|---|
| Embassy of Vietnam (Consular Section) | Jongno-gu, Seoul | Seoul, Gyeonggi, Gangwon, Chungcheong, Jeolla, Jeju |
| Vietnam Consulate General in Busan | Dong-gu, Busan | Busan, Gyeongnam, Daegu, Gyeongbuk, Ulsan |

> **Note:** Processing times may vary depending on the volume of applications and whether a verification inquiry to Vietnam is required. Always contact the consulate directly for the current processing time before applying.

<h2 id="section-4">Required Documents</h2>

The required documents may vary by consulate and individual circumstances. The following is a general list — always confirm with the consulate before applying:

| Document | Notes |
|---|---|
| Vietnamese Passport (original + copy) | Must be valid for at least 6 months |
| Vietnamese National ID (CCCD/CMND, original + copy) | Latest version |
| Vietnamese Household Registration (Hộ khẩu or Giấy xác nhận cư trú) | Issued within 3 months |
| Vietnamese Birth Certificate | Notarized copy may be required |
| Single Status Certificate (for unmarried applicants) | Issued by local People's Committee in Vietnam |
| Divorce/Death Certificate (if applicable) | Divorce judgment or death certificate |
| Application Form (consulate-designated format) | Available at the consulate or on their website |
| Application Fee | As announced by the consulate |

> **Tip:** Vietnamese documents (such as household registration) need to be prepared by family in Vietnam. Allow sufficient time as obtaining these documents can cause delays.

<h2 id="section-5">Step-by-Step Application Process</h2>

**Step 1:** Prepare Vietnamese documents (through family in Vietnam)

**Step 2:** Schedule appointment at the consulate (Seoul) or plan walk-in visit (Busan); complete the application form

**Step 3:** Visit the consulate, submit documents, and pay the fee

**Step 4:** Wait for processing (including possible Vietnam verification inquiry)

**Step 5:** Collect the certificate at the consulate (or inquire about postal delivery options)

**Step 6:** Obtain Korean translation and notarization for use in Korean marriage registration

---

<h2 id="section-6">Why No Apostille Is Required</h2>

Many people assume that foreign official documents always require an apostille. However, the Vietnam Certificate of Marriage Eligibility **does not require an apostille** when issued by the Vietnamese consulate in Korea, because the consulate itself is an authorized Vietnamese government body operating within Korea. The consular seal serves as official authentication.

> **Caution:** Vietnamese documents obtained directly from Vietnam (e.g., from a local People's Committee) may require an apostille or consular legalization before use in Korea. Requirements vary depending on where the document was issued.

<h2 id="section-7">Connecting to the F-6 Visa Application</h2>

After obtaining the Certificate of Marriage Eligibility, proceed as follows:

**1. Korean Marriage Registration**
- Submit the certificate (with Korean translation and notarization) + Vietnamese passport + Korean spouse's ID at the local district office
- Obtain the Korean Marriage Certificate (혼인관계증명서) after registration

**2. Vietnam Marriage Registration**
- Report the marriage to the local People's Committee in Vietnam using Korean marriage documents

**3. F-6-1 Visa Application**
- Apply at the Korean Embassy or Consulate in Vietnam
- Key documents: Korean Marriage Certificate, sponsor's income proof, evidence of genuine relationship

<h2 id="section-8">Frequently Asked Questions</h2>

**Q1. Does the certificate have an expiration date?**
Yes. It is generally valid for **6 months** from the date of issue. Complete the marriage registration and visa application as quickly as possible after obtaining it.

**Q2. Can a Busan resident apply at the Seoul consulate?**
In principle, you should apply at the consulate with jurisdiction over your place of residence. Busan residents should use the Busan Consulate General. Contact the consulate in advance if you have special circumstances.

**Q3. Can the Vietnamese spouse apply from overseas?**
Generally, the applicant must appear in person at the consulate. Whether a proxy application is possible depends on consulate policy — always inquire in advance.

**Q4. Are additional documents needed if the Vietnamese spouse was previously married?**
Yes. Proof of divorce or death of a previous spouse is required, along with Korean translation and notarization.

**Q5. Where do I get the Korean translation and notarization done?**
Translation must be done by a certified translator, and notarization is obtained at a Korean notary office or through a notary attorney. Vision Administrative Office can provide referrals.
