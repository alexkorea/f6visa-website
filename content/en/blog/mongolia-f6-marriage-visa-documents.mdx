<h2 id="section-1">F-6 Marriage Visa Eligibility Requirements for Mongolian Spouses</h2>

### Basic Eligibility Conditions

The F-6 Marriage Visa is a long-term residency status defined under Attached Table 1-2 of the Enforcement Decree of the Immigration Act, available to **spouses of Korean nationals** or **spouses of foreign nationals holding F-5 permanent residency**.
To apply for an F-6 visa as a Mongolian national, all of the following conditions must be met:

- A legally valid marriage to a **Korean national or F-5 permanent resident**
- Marriage registration completed in **both** Korea and Mongolia
- The sponsoring spouse (Korean national) meets the income and asset requirements
- Documentary evidence available to prove a genuine marital relationship

> **Important:** If marriage registration has only been completed in one country, you may receive a request for additional documents or experience delays in processing.
> Always confirm that the marriage has been registered in both countries before proceeding.

### Sponsor Income Requirements

The Korean sponsor must meet a minimum income or asset threshold.
The specific documents required for income verification vary depending on individual circumstances, and requests for supplementary income documentation have been increasing in recent cases.
**Since the income requirement criteria change annually, please consult a professional to confirm the current year's exact standards.**

<h2 id="section-3">Marriage Registration in Mongolia and Apostille Authentication</h2>

### Marriage Registration Procedure in Mongolia

Marriage registration in Mongolia is handled through the **Mongolian Civil Registration Authority**.
The **Marriage Certificate** issued after registration is the key document for the F-6 visa application.
Documents typically required for registration include:

- Original passports from both parties
- Korean marriage certificate (with Apostille)
- Birth certificates from both parties
- Declaration of intent to marry

### How to Obtain Apostille Certification

Mongolia is a member of the Hague Convention, so public documents can have their validity recognized abroad through **Apostille** certification.
The Apostille is processed through the issuing authority or the Mongolian Ministry of Justice.
After obtaining the Apostille, you must additionally complete a **certified Korean translation** of the documents.

> **Practical tip:** Apostille-certified documents may be subject to re-issuance requests if too much time has passed since the issue date.
> It is advisable to obtain documents as close to your visa application date as possible.

### Marriage Registration in Korea

For marriage registration in Korea, the Korean spouse files at the district, city, or township office with jurisdiction over their registered domicile or current address.
Required documents include the marriage registration form, identification from both parties, the Mongolian Marriage Certificate (with translation and notarization), and birth certificates.
Once the Korean marriage registration is complete, the spouse will be listed in the Korean family registry, fulfilling the F-6 visa application requirement.

> **Important:** If the translation or notarization of Mongolian documents does not meet the required standards, the marriage registration itself may be rejected at the intake stage.
> It is strongly recommended to inquire with the relevant district or city office in advance, or to have a professional review your documents before preparing them.

<h2 id="section-4">F-6 Visa Application Process (Based on Application in Mongolia)</h2>

### Step-by-Step Application Flow

If the Mongolian spouse is currently in Mongolia, they will typically apply for the F-6 visa directly at the **Embassy of the Republic of Korea in Mongolia**.

| Step | Description | Estimated Time |
|------|-------------|----------------|
| Step 1 | Complete marriage registration in both countries | 1–4 weeks |
| Step 2 | Collect documents, obtain Apostille, complete certified translation | 2–4 weeks |
| Step 3 | Submit F-6 visa application at the Korean Embassy in Mongolia | From date of submission |
| Step 4 | Visa screening and result notification | Several weeks to several months (varies by case) |
| Step 5 | Enter Korea after visa is issued | From date of issuance |
| Step 6 | Register as a foreigner within 90 days of entry | At the local immigration office |

> **Practical tip:** Processing times vary by embassy and immigration office, and additional time is required if supplementary documents are requested.
> To proceed as quickly as possible, consult a professional in advance to check current processing times.

### Changing Residency Status from Within Korea

If the Mongolian spouse is already in Korea on a short-term visa or other status, they may apply for a **change of residency status** at the local immigration office.
However, eligibility and requirements depend on the type of visa currently held.
**Whether this option applies to your specific situation must be confirmed by a professional.**

Detailed procedures are also available on the official [HiKorea](https://www.hikorea.go.kr) or [Korea Immigration Service](https://www.immigration.go.kr) websites.

<h2 id="section-5">Key Considerations During the Mongolia Marriage Visa Screening</h2>

### Evidence of a Genuine Marital Relationship

Immigration authorities place significant emphasis on verifying a **genuine marital relationship** during the F-6 visa screening, beyond the formal documents.
The duration of the relationship, means of communication, and frequency of meetings may all be reviewed.
Preparing the following materials will strengthen your application:

- Photos taken together (especially helpful if date and location can be confirmed)
- Wedding photos or video footage
- Records of communication (messages, call logs, etc.)
- Flight tickets and accommodation records for visits
- International call or video call history

### Stricter Scrutiny Against Fraudulent Marriages

Screening standards to prevent fraudulent marriages have been tightened in recent years.
You may be asked to attend an interview or submit additional documents, and consistent statements throughout the process are critical.
**Since screening criteria have recently changed in part, the exact preparation required for your situation must be confirmed by a professional.**

> **Practical tip:** If the relationship period is short or language communication is limited, the likelihood of additional documentation requests increases.
> In such cases, working with a professional to prepare documents systematically gives you the best chance of passing the screening.

<h2 id="section-6">FAQ — Frequently Asked Questions About the Mongolia Marriage Visa</h2>

**Q1. How long does the F-6 visa process take for a Mongolian spouse?**

A. Processing time varies depending on the application route (embassy or domestic immigration office), whether all documents are complete, and whether additional screening is triggered.
Embassy applications typically take anywhere from several weeks to several months.
Since processing times differ by embassy and immigration office, please consult a professional for current timelines.

**Q2. Can the F-6 visa be obtained without completing marriage registration in Mongolia?**

A. In principle, marriage registration must be completed in **both Korea and Mongolia**.
If only the Korean registration has been completed without registering in Mongolia, the visa may be denied or the application may be disadvantaged during screening.
Always confirm that marriage registration is complete in both countries before applying.

**Q3. What happens if the sponsor does not meet the income requirements?**

A. Failure to meet the sponsor's income requirements is one of the most common grounds for F-6 visa denial.
In some cases, alternative approaches such as providing asset documentation or factoring in the number of dependents may be possible, but this varies by situation.

**Q5. Do Mongolian-language documents need to be translated into Korean?**

A. Yes, a **certified Korean translation** is mandatory.
The translation must be completed by a certified translator or notary as required under the Notary Act, which can be referenced on the [National Law Information Center](https://www.law.go.kr) website.
Documents translated by unqualified individuals may be rejected at the submission stage.

**Q6. Is it possible to work after entering Korea on an F-6 visa?**

A. The F-6 Marriage Immigration status generally permits employment activities without requiring a separate work permit.
However, residency status conditions and relevant laws are subject to change, so always check the latest information on the official [Korea Immigration Service](https://www.immigration.go.kr) website or consult a professional.
