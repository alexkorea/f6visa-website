# F-6 Marriage Visa Document Checklist 2026 Korea (All Types)

The documents required for an F-6 marriage immigration visa vary by type. Based on Article 9-2 of the Enforcement Regulations of the Immigration Act and the detailed standards for visa application documents, here is the 2026 document checklist.

## 1. Overview of F-6 Visa Types

The F-6 marriage immigration visa is divided into three subtypes:

- **F-6-1**: Spouse of a Korean national
- **F-6-2**: Father or mother raising a child born in a marriage with a Korean national, as recognized by the Minister of Justice
- **F-6-3**: Person raising minor children domestically after divorce or bereavement from a Korean citizen spouse

## 2. Common Required Documents (All Types)

The following documents are required for all F-6 visa types:

- [ ] **Visa application form** (Ministry of Justice format)
- [ ] **Passport** (valid for at least 6 months, including a copy)
- [ ] **Standard-format photo** (3.5cm × 4.5cm, taken within the last 6 months)
- [ ] **Fee payment receipt**

## 3. F-6-1 (Spouse of Korean National) Required Documents

F-6-1 is the most common type, for foreign nationals legally married to a Korean citizen.

**Applicant's documents:**
- [ ] Marriage certificate (issued within 3 months, with Apostille or consular authentication)
- [ ] Wedding photos (2–3 photos from the ceremony)
- [ ] Family relationship certificate (if applicable)
- [ ] Criminal background check (issued by police in applicant's country, with Apostille)

**Sponsor's (spouse's) documents:**
- [ ] Invitation letter (Ministry of Justice format)
- [ ] Personal guarantee letter (Ministry of Justice format)
- [ ] Resident registration certificate (issued within 3 months)
- [ ] Family relationship certificate (issued within 3 months)
- [ ] Income verification documents (withholding tax receipt, health insurance payment confirmation, etc.)
- [ ] Employment certificate or business registration certificate
- [ ] Marriage certificate

> **Note**: Additional documents may be required depending on whether the International Marriage Guidance Program (pre-education) has been completed. For certain countries (Vietnam, China, Philippines, Cambodia, Mongolia, Uzbekistan, etc.), this pre-education is mandatory.

## 4. F-6-2 (Child-Rearing Parent) Required Documents

Applicable when raising a child born in a marriage with a Korean national.

- [ ] Child's birth certificate or family relationship certificate
- [ ] Documents confirming child-rearing (resident registration certificate including child, child support payment records, etc.)
- [ ] Divorce or bereavement documents (divorce judgment, death certificate, etc.)
- [ ] Resident registration certificate (including the child)

## 5. F-6-3 (Child-Rearing After Divorce/Bereavement) Required Documents

Applicable when raising minor children domestically after divorce or bereavement from a Korean citizen spouse.

- [ ] Divorce judgment or mutual divorce confirmation / death certificate
- [ ] Child custody documents (court ruling or agreement)
- [ ] Child's family relationship certificate
- [ ] Documents confirming current child-rearing status (school enrollment certificate, health insurance documentation, etc.)

## 6. Important Notes on Document Preparation

- **Translation and notarization**: Documents in a foreign language must be accompanied by a Korean-language notarized translation.
- **Apostille or consular authentication**: If the country is a signatory to the Hague Convention, an Apostille is required; for non-signatory countries, consular authentication from the Korean embassy or consulate in that country is needed.
- **Validity period**: Most certificates must have been issued within 3 months.
- **Original documents**: If submitting copies, you must bring the originals for verification.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q. Can I apply if the marriage registration has not yet been completed?**
A. The F-6-1 visa requires a legally valid marriage in both countries. The marriage registration in Korea or the official registration in the other country must be completed before application.

**Q. What happens if documents are missing?**
A. If documents are missing, the review may be suspended or you may be asked to submit additional documents. Since some overseas missions only provide one opportunity, it is important to have all documents ready at initial submission.

**Q. Where can I obtain a criminal background check?**
A. It is issued by the police agency or relevant government authority in your country of residence and requires Apostille authentication followed by notarized translation. In Korea, the National Police Agency's Criminal Record Inquiry Service (Minwon 24) can be used.
