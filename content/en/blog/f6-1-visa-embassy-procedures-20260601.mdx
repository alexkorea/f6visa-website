# F-6-1 Visa: Embassy Application Procedures by Country

## Overview

The F-6-1 (Spouse of Korean National) visa is applied for at the Korean embassy or consulate in the applicant's home country. While core documents are similar across countries, local certification requirements vary.

## Common Required Documents

- Visa application form
- Passport (original and copy)
- One passport photo
- Marriage certificate (locally notarized + Korean translation notarized)
- Korean spouse's marriage registration certificate
- Korean spouse's family relations certificate
- Letter of invitation and identity guarantee from Korean spouse
- Financial proof from Korean spouse

## Vietnam Embassy Procedures

Apply at the Korean Embassy in Hanoi or the Korean Consulate General in Ho Chi Minh City. The Vietnamese marriage certificate issued by the People's Committee must be translated into Korean and notarized. Embassy interview required; processing time is typically 4–8 weeks.

## China Embassy Procedures

Apply at consulates in Beijing, Shanghai, Guangzhou, Shenyang, Qingdao, or Chengdu. The Chinese marriage certificate must be notarized by a notary office and authenticated by the Ministry of Foreign Affairs. Processing time is typically 4–6 weeks.

## Philippines Embassy Procedures

Apply at the Korean Embassy in Manila. The PSA-issued Marriage Certificate must have an apostille and be translated into Korean.
