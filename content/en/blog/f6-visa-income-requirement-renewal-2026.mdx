When renewing an F-6 marriage immigration visa, failing to meet the income standard can create challenges during the review process.

Under **Immigration Control Act Enforcement Rules**, financial self-sufficiency is a core review factor for F-6 renewal. The income threshold is linked to the standard median income announced annually by the Ministry of Health and Welfare.

This guide explains the F-6 renewal income requirement, how to use a guarantor, and what to do when income falls short — updated for 2026.

## What is the F-6 Renewal Income Requirement?

When reviewing an F-6 renewal, immigration authorities assess whether the Korean spouse can financially support the household.

The 2026 income standard is generally 80% or more of the standard median income adjusted for household size. Since this threshold changes annually, always verify the latest standard before applying.

Ways to prove income:
- **Employment income:** Withholding tax receipt, certificate of employment, salary bank statements
- **Business income:** Business income declaration, VAT filing
- **Other income:** Rental income, financial income with supporting documents
- **Foreign spouse's income:** The foreign spouse's income can be combined with the Korean spouse's to meet the standard

## Using a Guarantor When Income Falls Short

If the Korean spouse's income does not meet the standard, a **personal guarantor** can be used for the renewal application.

**Guarantor requirements:**
- Adult Korean national
- Income or assets above a certain level
- Legal capacity to fulfill guarantor obligations

The guarantor legally assumes responsibility if the foreign spouse encounters legal or financial difficulties during their stay. Family members (parents-in-law, etc.) are most commonly used as guarantors.

## When Can You Renew Without a Guarantor?

Renewal without a guarantor is possible when:
- **Korean spouse's income meets the threshold:** 80%+ of standard median income based on household size
- **Combined income qualifies:** The employed foreign spouse's income is combined to meet the standard
- **Asset-based proof:** Sufficient real estate or financial assets are held instead of income

Even when income falls short, factors like assets, marital stability (children, years of marriage), and social ties are considered holistically.

## Cases Where Income Requirements Are Relaxed

In certain circumstances the income requirement may be reduced or waived:
- **Pregnancy or new infant:** Understanding may be applied for household income difficulties during pregnancy/early childcare
- **Death of Korean spouse:** Status change from F-6-1 to F-6-3 (child custody) undergoes separate review
- **Post-divorce child custody:** Separate review criteria apply for F-6 status maintenance

Each situation requires different documents and review criteria. In special circumstances, always consult a specialist before applying.

## Income Checklist for F-6 Renewal

- [ ] Confirm household size (Korean spouse, children)
- [ ] Check current year's standard median income threshold
- [ ] Prepare income proof documents (recent 1–3 months' payslips, withholding tax receipt)
- [ ] If income falls short, secure guarantor and prepare guarantor's documents
- [ ] Verify whether foreign spouse's income can be combined
- [ ] Confirm renewal deadline (recommend 4 months before expiry)

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q1. Is F-6 renewal impossible if the Korean spouse is unemployed?**

Not impossible. Even if unemployed, renewal may be possible using a guarantor or by demonstrating sufficient assets. Evidence of active job search can also be considered.

**Q2. Can the foreign spouse's income count toward the income standard?**

Yes. The employed foreign spouse's income can be combined. Submit the foreign spouse's withholding tax receipt together.

**Q3. Does the income standard change every year?**

Yes. The standard median income is updated annually by Ministry of Health and Welfare notice. Always verify the latest standard before applying.

**Q4. Does the guarantor have to be a family member?**

Not necessarily. Any adult Korean national meeting the legal requirements can serve as guarantor. In practice, family members are most common.

**Q5. If renewal is denied due to income, what can I do?**

Re-application or appeal is possible. Identify the reason for denial, then re-apply after adding a guarantor or supplementing income proof. Working with a specialist significantly improves success rates.
