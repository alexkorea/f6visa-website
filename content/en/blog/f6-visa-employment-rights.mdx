# Can You Work in Korea on an F-6 Marriage Visa?

**The short answer: yes — F-6 visa holders can work in Korea without a separate work permit.** F-6 is one of the few visa categories with no employment restrictions, meaning you can change employers or industries without any special reporting. Understanding the details, however, helps you avoid misunderstandings or complications.

<h2 id="section-1">F-6 Visa and Employment</h2>

Under the Immigration Control Act, the F-6 status has **no employment restrictions**. This means:

- No separate work permit (고용허가서) required
- Employers do not need to file a prior notification for hiring you
- No industry or job-type restrictions
- Part-time, full-time, and contract work all permitted

The only exception is certain positions that are legally barred to foreigners (e.g., some government positions), which apply regardless of visa type.

<h2 id="section-2">What Jobs Can You Do?</h2>

F-6 holders can work in virtually any sector — the same as Korean nationals. Common examples:

| Sector | Examples |
|--------|---------|
| Service | Restaurants, cafes, convenience stores |
| Office | Corporate roles, IT, marketing, translation |
| Education | Language academy instructor, tutoring |
| Manufacturing | Factory line work |
| Professional | Doctor, lawyer (with Korean license) |
| Freelance | Translation, design, video editing |

<h2 id="section-3">Important Considerations</h2>

**Written employment contract:** Always sign a written contract. It protects you in wage disputes or wrongful termination cases.

**Minimum wage applies:** The minimum wage applies equally to foreign workers. As of 2025 it is KRW 10,030/hour.

**Income tax withholding:** Employment income is subject to withholding tax (3.3% for freelancers; standard income tax table for employees). Year-end tax refunds are possible.

**No activity reporting needed:** Because F-6 has no employment restrictions, you do not need to file an "activities outside status" notification.

<h2 id="section-4">Starting a Business</h2>

F-6 visa holders may also register a sole proprietorship or participate in a corporation.

- **Sole proprietorship:** Register with your local tax office; F-6 holders are eligible.
- **Corporation:** You may serve as director or CEO.
- **D-8 Investor Visa option:** If you invest KRW 100 million or more, switching to a D-8 investment visa provides greater visa renewal stability based on investment track record.

> **Note:** You can maintain F-6 status even after starting a business — switching to D-8 is optional.

<h2 id="section-5">Social Insurance Coverage</h2>

When employed, you are enrolled in Korea's four social insurance programs:

| Insurance | F-6 Applicability |
|-----------|------------------|
| National Health Insurance | Yes |
| National Pension | Yes (exemptions may apply by treaty) |
| Employment Insurance | Yes |
| Industrial Accident Insurance | Yes |

If you were already enrolled as a local subscriber (지역가입자), employment switches you to a workplace subscriber (직장가입자), which is generally cheaper.

<h2 id="section-6">Effect on Visa Renewal</h2>

Employment can actually help your F-6 visa renewal by demonstrating financial self-sufficiency:

- Include pay stubs and employment certificates when renewing
- If your spouse's income is low, your own income can be used as evidence of financial stability

<h2 id="section-7">FAQ</h2>

**Q. Can I teach English on an F-6 visa?**
A. Yes. F-6 has no employment restrictions, so teaching at private academies is fully permitted. Note that public school native-speaker positions (requiring E-2 visa) have separate eligibility criteria.

**Q. Can I work for two employers simultaneously?**
A. Yes. F-6 allows multiple employers with no reporting required.

**Q. Does my work affect my spouse's F-6 visa?**
A. No. As long as you maintain F-6 status, your employment activity does not affect your visa eligibility.

**Q. Do I need to register a business if I freelance?**
A. Registering as a sole proprietor is recommended for ongoing freelance work. You can file comprehensive income taxes without registration, but registration simplifies tax management.
