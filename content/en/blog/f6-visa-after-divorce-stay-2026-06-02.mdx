## F-6 Visa and Divorce

The F-6 (Marriage Immigration) visa is tied to a valid marriage with a Korean national. After divorce, the visa basis changes — but automatic deportation does not always follow.

## Conditions to Remain After Divorce

Under the Immigration Control Act Article 25-2, you may change your status if:

- Raising a Korean-national child born during the marriage
- Divorced due to the Korean spouse's fault (domestic violence, abuse)
- Married for 2+ years with proof of genuine cohabitation

## Status Change Procedure

After divorce is finalized, apply for a status change at your local Immigration Office.

Key documents:
- Divorce judgment or divorce registration certificate
- Child custody documents (if applicable)
- Proof of marriage duration (family register, photos)
- Evidence of spouse's fault (if applicable: medical record, police report)

## Available Statuses

Depending on circumstances: F-1 (visiting family), F-6-3 (post-divorce/widowed), or naturalization application.
